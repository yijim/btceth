Lesson 1: What is Bitcoin?

Time: 15 min · Last updated: 2025-11-14

1. Why do we even need Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is the first successful decentralized digital currency. Instead of relying on banks or governments, it uses a public ledger called the blockchain to record all transactions.

A simple way to imagine Bitcoin: a global spreadsheet that everyone can check, but no single person can secretly change.

2. Key ideas in one picture

  • No central bank – rules are defined by open-source code.
  • Limited supply – only 21 million BTC will ever exist.
  • Anyone can verify transactions – transparency by design.

This combination makes Bitcoin very different from traditional money, which can be printed in unlimited quantities.

3. Bitcoin vs. the coins you see on exchanges

Most of the coins you see today (altcoins, meme coins) are built after Bitcoin. Many are experiments; some are pure hype.

If you first understand Bitcoin's purpose and design, you can better judge whether a new coin is innovating something real, or just borrowing marketing buzzwords.

⚠️ Risk Reminder

These notes are for education only. Nothing here is financial advice. Crypto is highly volatile. Never invest money you cannot afford to lose.

4. Quick recap

  • Bitcoin = first decentralized digital currency.
  • Blockchain = shared ledger that everyone can verify.
  • Limited supply + transparent rules make it unique.

In the next lesson, we will zoom out from Bitcoin and look at other public blockchains (Ethereum, Solana, etc.) and how they extend the ideas introduced here.
