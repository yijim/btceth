SOL Meme Flow & Volume Notes

Date: 2025-11-15 · Draft · Chain: Solana

1. What I looked at

This note is not a tutorial, but a record of how I observe Solana meme coin flow and try to understand the relationship between volume, liquidity, entries, and risk.

For this round of notes, I mainly watched:

  • New meme launches on Solana (Raydium / Jupiter)
  • Top meme pairs by 24h volume
  • Liquidity (LP size) vs fully diluted valuation (FDV)
  • Price reaction around first 30–60 minutes after launch

2. Simple observations

一些我自己在盘中和复盘中发现的“特点”：

  • High volume + thin liquidity: A lot of new memes have decent 5–10m volume but extremely thin LP. Price moves are very sharp, both up and down.
  • First 1–3 candles are often pure emotion: Early candles are driven by pure FOMO. If I enter too late in this phase, I usually end up exit liquidity.
  • Twitter hype lags price: By the time a meme starts trending on CT, the best R:R is mostly gone.

3. Volume vs liquidity

One simple pattern: when 24h volume is big, but liquidity is still tiny, price is very sensitive to even medium-sized orders.

Practically, this means:

  • Buying big size early is dangerous — you move the price yourself.
  • It's easy to get trapped if LP is not growing while volume keeps rotating.
  • Many “strong” charts are just thin books and aggressive market orders.

My internal rule: if LP is too small compared to the move I want, I either reduce size or skip completely. “Looks good” is not enough without liquidity.

4. Entry timing thoughts

Instead of trying to “snipe the absolute bottom”, I focus on:

  • Waiting for at least some structure (higher low, small consolidation)
  • Avoiding entry on the biggest, strongest green candle
  • Watching whether LP is being added or silently pulled

If I missed a clean entry and price already went 5–10x, I'm learning to simply let it go instead of forcing a late FOMO entry.

5. How I adjust my own trading

  • Keep size small (fixed, like 0.1 SOL per try).
  • Respect LP & volume — no entry if they don't make sense.
  • Prefer memes where liquidity is gradually increasing, not static.
  • Use clear invalidation: if structure breaks or volume dies, I exit.

6. Open questions for future notes

Some things I want to track further in later notes:

  • How often do memes with early strong LP survive longer than a few days?
  • What is the win-rate if I only trade memes with locked liquidity?
  • How does entry timing + first tweet / first CT hype relate to R:R?

These are not conclusions, just directions for the next round of data and journaling.
